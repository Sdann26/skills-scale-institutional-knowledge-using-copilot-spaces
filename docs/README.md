# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management Documentation! This README provides an overview of the project management processes used at OctoAcme, as well as quick links to all detailed process documents.

## Summary of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme uses an iterative, customer-focused approach for project delivery, emphasizing clear ownership, transparency, and continuous improvement. Our project lifecycle is structured around six core phases:

### **Initiation**
Validate ideas, align stakeholders, define success metrics, and create initial plans. This phase ensures that we understand the business need, identify key stakeholders, and establish clear success criteria before committing resources to planning.

### **Planning**
Break work into shippable increments, estimate scope, identify risks and dependencies, and establish a release timeline. Planning phase outputs include a prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria and a clear Definition of Done.

### **Execution & Tracking**
Execute work through organized sprints or iterations, track progress via project boards and metrics, ensure quality through defined workflows and testing strategies, and escalate blockers efficiently. Daily standups and weekly syncs keep the team aligned and risks visible.

### **Release & Deployment**
Standardize how we release features to production with pre-release checklists, deployment procedures, rollback plans, and clear communication. This phase reduces risk and improves observability during deployments.

### **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement**
Capture learnings after each sprint, release, or milestone. Convert insights into actionable improvements, assign owners, track closure, and celebrate successes to foster a culture of continuous learning.

### **Risk Management & Communication**
Regularly review and manage risks using a centralized Risk Register. Communicate status and decisions consistently to stakeholders using defined templates and escalation paths to ensure visibility and alignment.

### **Defined Roles & Personas**
Clear role definitions and responsibilities for Project Managers, Product Managers, Developers, QA/Testing, and Stakeholders. This clarity ensures smooth collaboration and accountability across teams.

## Core Principles

- **Customer-first**: Prioritize customer value and usability in all decisions.
- **Iterative delivery**: Deliver small, testable increments to gather feedback early.
- **Clear ownership**: Each project has a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Lead.
- **Data-informed decisions**: Measure impact and iterate based on evidence.
- **Psychological safety**: Encourage feedback, learning, and continuous improvement.
