# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Core Delivery Roles

### Developers

#### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

#### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

#### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

#### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

#### Key Interactions
- Works with Product Managers on acceptance criteria and prioritization
- Collaborates with Project Managers on scheduling and blockers
- Partners with UX Designers on implementation details and usability
- Coordinates with Security Engineers on security requirements
- Supports Customer Support Lead with documentation and readiness

---

### Product Managers

#### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

#### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

#### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

#### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

#### Key Interactions
- Aligns with Project Managers on scope and timelines
- Works with Developers on feasibility and technical trade-offs
- Partners with UX Designers on user needs and design validation
- Incorporates input from Business Analysts on stakeholder requirements
- Collaborates with Customer Support Lead on customer feedback and readiness

---

### Project Managers

#### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

#### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

#### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

#### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

#### Key Interactions
- Partners with Product Managers on backlog and release planning
- Coordinates with Developers on sprint delivery and blockers
- Works with Security Engineers to track security risk mitigation
- Collaborates with Business Analysts on stakeholder alignment
- Engages Customer Support Lead for deployment and communication readiness

---

## Extended Cross-Functional Roles

### UX Designer

#### Role Summary
UX Designers own the user experience quality and ensure that features are intuitive, accessible, and meet user needs. They advocate for usability throughout the project lifecycle and collaborate closely with Product and Development teams.

#### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and translate insights into design requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Lead design reviews and ensure consistency with design systems
- Collaborate with Developers on implementation fidelity
- Test designs with users and iterate based on feedback
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design practices

#### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support costs
- Ensure feature adoption and user satisfaction
- Maintain design consistency across the product

#### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with Developers and Product Managers
- User research findings and design rationale documentation
- Feedback loops on implementation quality and usability testing
- Collaboration in sprint planning and retrospectives

#### Key Interactions
- Works with Product Managers to understand user needs and prioritize features
- Partners with Developers on design implementation and quality assurance
- Supports Project Managers by identifying UX-related risks and dependencies
- Advises Security Engineers on user-centric security UX (e.g., authentication flows)
- Consults with Business Analysts on user requirements and personas

---

### Security Engineer

#### Role Summary
Security Engineers ensure that projects meet security requirements and risk mitigation standards. They review designs, code, and deployment plans to identify vulnerabilities and enforce secure practices throughout the project lifecycle.

#### Responsibilities
- Define security requirements and acceptance criteria for projects
- Review architectural designs and code for security vulnerabilities
- Coordinate security testing and vulnerability scanning in CI
- Advise on secure deployment and incident response procedures
- Track and monitor security risks in the project risk register
- Ensure compliance with organizational security policies and standards

#### Goals
- Prevent security incidents and data breaches
- Integrate security early in the development lifecycle (shift-left)
- Maintain customer trust and organizational compliance
- Enable secure, rapid deployment

#### Typical Communication
- Security design reviews and threat modeling sessions
- Security scanning reports and vulnerability assessments
- Risk register updates and mitigation status
- Incident response planning and post-incident reviews

#### Key Interactions
- Advises Product Managers on security trade-offs and compliance needs
- Works with Developers on secure coding practices and vulnerability remediation
- Partners with Project Managers to track security risks and escalate issues
- Collaborates with UX Designers on secure user authentication and data privacy UX
- Supports Business Analysts in understanding regulatory and compliance requirements

---

### Business Analyst

#### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical execution. They translate stakeholder requirements into actionable items, clarify acceptance criteria, and ensure alignment between business goals and project outcomes.

#### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into functional and technical specifications
- Clarify acceptance criteria and success metrics with stakeholders
- Identify business process changes and training needs
- Support scope management and change control
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business requirements

#### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Minimize scope creep and rework through clear requirements
- Accelerate stakeholder alignment and decision-making
- Support successful adoption and change management

#### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and specification reviews
- Stakeholder interviews and elicitation sessions
- Backlog refinement and acceptance criteria definition
- Change request evaluation and impact analysis

#### Key Interactions
- Partners with Product Managers on business goal definition and prioritization
- Works with Project Managers on scope, change management, and stakeholder communication
- Collaborates with Developers on technical feasibility and requirement clarification
- Supports UX Designers in understanding business process impacts and user personas
- Advises Security Engineers on compliance and regulatory requirements

---

### Customer Support Lead

#### Role Summary
Customer Support Leads ensure that released features are properly communicated, documented, and supported. They maintain the feedback loop between customers and the product team, informing prioritization and identifying readiness issues before launch.

#### Responsibilities
- Coordinate with the team on feature documentation and support training
- Gather post-launch customer feedback and issue reports
- Identify support readiness issues before deployment
- Document known issues and workarounds
- Partner with Product Managers to prioritize customer-reported issues
- Facilitate communication between customers and the delivery team

#### Goals
- Ensure smooth customer adoption and minimal support escalations
- Reduce time-to-resolution for customer issues
- Capture customer insights to inform product improvements
- Maintain high customer satisfaction

#### Typical Communication
- Pre-release readiness checklists and support documentation
- Customer feedback summaries and issue reports
- Post-launch metrics on adoption, errors, and support volume
- Collaboration in retrospectives to capture lessons learned

#### Key Interactions
- Works with Product Managers to prioritize customer-reported issues and feedback
- Partners with Developers on documentation, troubleshooting guides, and known issues
- Collaborates with Project Managers on release communication and deployment readiness
- Advises UX Designers on usability pain points and support patterns
- Supports Business Analysts in understanding customer adoption and change management needs

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When planning projects, ensure all relevant personas are identified and their responsibilities clearly assigned.
- Use the "Key Interactions" section to clarify collaboration expectations and reduce handoff friction.
